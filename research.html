    Our Approach

    Research at the Electrical Machines Laboratory

    Research at the Electrical Machines Laboratory is grounded in a simple conviction: the most valuable engineering knowledge comes from the tension between mathematical models and physical experiments. We build theoretical frameworks, validate them rigorously on laboratory hardware, and calibrate them against real machine behaviour.

    Our problem-driven approach begins with challenges that industry practitioners and infrastructure operators face — motors that fail unexpectedly, drives that operate inefficiently, generators that do not integrate cleanly with renewable-heavy grids. We work backward from those problems to the fundamental electromagnetic, thermal, and mechanical phenomena that govern them.

    The laboratory maintains simultaneous research programmes across six core domains, with ongoing projects funded by national agencies including DST-SERB and AICTE, and through sponsored research agreements with industrial partners. Every project involves both faculty research leadership and active participation by doctoral and postgraduate students, ensuring that research productivity and research education reinforce one another.

    Core Investigations

    Research Areas

    Six core domains of investigation spanning machine design, control, diagnostics, and sustainable energy systems.

    Electric Machine Design

    Electromagnetic optimisation via FEM and analytical modelling
    High-efficiency induction and synchronous machine design
    Thermal analysis and cooling system design
    Rotor geometry and stator slot shape optimisation

    Special Electrical Machines

    Permanent Magnet Synchronous Machines (PMSM / IPMSM)
    Brushless DC (BLDC) motor design and drive
    Switched Reluctance Machines (SRM) — design and control
    Axial flux and transverse flux machine topologies

    Condition Monitoring

    Motor Current Signature Analysis (MCSA) for fault detection
    Bearing, rotor bar, and winding insulation fault diagnosis
    Vibration-based signal analysis and classification
    ML and deep-learning-based predictive maintenance

    Motor Drives & Control

    Field-Oriented Control (FOC) and Direct Torque Control (DTC)
    Model Predictive Control (MPC) for variable-speed drives
    Sensorless speed and position estimation
    Adaptive and robust control under parameter variations

    Power Electronics

    Wide-bandgap devices: SiC and GaN converter design
    Multilevel inverter topologies — NPC and cascaded H-bridge
    EMI characterisation and mitigation strategies
    High-frequency DC-DC converter design for EV chargers

    Sustainable Electrification

    EV traction motor and drivetrain optimisation
    Regenerative braking and battery-motor co-optimisation
    DFIG and PMSG-based wind energy systems
    Microgrid stability and smart grid integration

    Featured Research Areas

    Major investigations currently shaping the laboratory's research agenda.

    Machine Design

    FEM-Based Optimisation of Interior Permanent Magnet Machines

    This research programme develops systematic finite-element methods for designing IPMSM rotors that maximise torque density while minimising ripple, iron loss, and thermal stress. We combine parametric FEM sweeps with gradient-based and evolutionary optimisation algorithms to navigate the high-dimensional design space efficiently.

    Objectives
    Torque density >25 Nm/kg · Ripple <3% · Efficiency >96%
    Methods
    2D/3D FEM · RSM optimisation · Thermal-coupled analysis
    Applications
    EV traction · Industrial drives · Servo actuators
    Faculty
    Prof. R. Kumar · Dr. S. Mohanty
    FEM IPMSM Optimisation Thermal Analysis
    Recent Output: "Optimal Stator Slot Shape Design for Torque Ripple Minimisation in IPMSM" — IEEE Trans. Energy Conv., 2024
    Condition Monitoring

    Non-Invasive Fault Diagnosis Using Motor Current Signature Analysis

    This programme develops signal processing and machine learning methods for detecting bearing faults, broken rotor bars, and winding insulation degradation in squirrel cage induction motors during normal operation — with no physical access to the machine required.

    Objectives
    Early fault detection · Classification accuracy >95% · Online deployment
    Methods
    FFT / STFT / Wavelet · SVM · CNN-based classifiers
    Applications
    Industrial motors · Pump drives · Critical process equipment
    Faculty
    Prof. R. Kumar · Dr. A. Nanda
    MCSA Wavelet-SVM CNN Predictive Maintenance
    Recent Output: "Online Broken Rotor Bar Detection Using Wavelet-SVM" — Mech. Syst. Signal Process., 2024
    Motor Drives

    Model Predictive Torque Control for Three-Level NPC Inverter-Fed Drives

    This investigation develops finite-control-set MPC algorithms that reduce switching frequency and torque ripple in NPC inverter-fed PMSM drives without sacrificing dynamic response. Online parameter estimation ensures robustness under temperature and load variation.

    Objectives
    Switching freq. reduction ≥40% · THD <3% · Transient response <5ms
    Methods
    FCS-MPC · Online parameter estimation · dSPACE HIL validation
    Applications
    High-power drives · Traction · Renewable energy converters
    Faculty
    Dr. S. Mohanty · Prof. R. Kumar
    MPC NPC Inverter PMSM dSPACE
    Recent Output: "MPTC with Reduced Switching for 3L-NPC Fed PMSM" — IEEE Trans. Ind. Electron., 2023

    Completed Projects

    Projects that have concluded and their principal outcomes and scholarly contributions.

    2020 – 2023
    Sensorless FOC of Induction Motor Drive for Pump Applications

    Developed a model reference adaptive system (MRAS)-based sensorless FOC algorithm for induction motor pumping systems, achieving accurate speed estimation down to 2 Hz. Validated on 5 kW laboratory test bench with DSP implementation.

    3 Journal Papers 2 IEEE Conferences 1 PhD Awarded
    2018 – 2022
    DFIG-Based Wind Energy Conversion Under Grid Voltage Unbalance — DST Fast Track

    Investigated novel dual-current-controller strategies for DFIG operation under unbalanced grid conditions, suppressing second-harmonic oscillations in DC-link power while maintaining rated MPPT efficiency. Funded by DST Fast Track (Young Scientist) scheme.

    4 Journal Papers 3 Conferences Industry Transfer
    2016 – 2020
    Condition Monitoring of Squirrel Cage Induction Motors via MCSA — DST SERB

    Established MCSA-based fault detection protocols for bearing, broken bar, and eccentricity faults. Developed a wavelet-transformed SVM classifier achieving 97.3% classification accuracy across five fault conditions on an instrumented 7.5 kW test rig.

    5 Journal Papers 4 Conferences 2 PhDs Awarded 1 Patent Filed
    2014 – 2018
    Optimum Design of Energy-Efficient Three-Phase Induction Motors — BHEL Sponsored

    Collaborated with BHEL Bhopal to redesign stator slot geometry and winding configuration of standard frame motors, achieving IE3 efficiency class compliance with no increase in frame size. Laboratory prototype tested and validated against BHEL's test bed results.

    2 Journal Papers BHEL Technology Transfer 1 PhD Awarded
