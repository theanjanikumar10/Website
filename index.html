Domains of Inquiry

Research Areas

Four core domains of investigation driving innovation in electric power and machine technology.

Electric Machines

Design, analysis, and optimisation of high-efficiency induction motors, permanent magnet machines, synchronous reluctance machines, and special-purpose electric motors using FEM and analytical methods.

Electric Vehicles

Traction motor design, regenerative braking systems, battery-motor co-optimisation, and thermal management for next-generation EV powertrains — from two-wheelers to heavy traction.

Motor Control & Drives

Advanced control algorithms including field-oriented control, direct torque control, and model-predictive control for precision variable-speed drives, sensorless operation, and real-time embedded implementation.

Power Electronics

Wide-bandgap device applications (SiC, GaN), multilevel converter topologies, EMI mitigation, and high-frequency power conversion systems for motor drives and energy-conversion applications.

From the Lab In-Charge

A Message from Laboratory Head

"Electric machines are not merely electrical to mechanical energy conversion devices — they are the silent engines of our sustainable future. Every watt saved in a motor, every kilogram reduced in a traction drive, every millisecond shaved from a control response brings us measurably closer to a decarbonised world."

The global transition to sustainable energy is not an abstract challenge — it is an engineering problem, and electric machines sit at its very centre. Whether it is the traction motor in an EV replacing a combustion engine, the wind generator feeding clean power into the grid, or the industrial drive that cuts a factory's energy bill by a third, the quality of our machine design and control directly determines how fast and how far that transition goes.

Our research group at IIT Bhubaneswar is focused on the machines and drives that make this possible. We work on synchronous reluctance machines, permanent magnet motors, and advanced power electronic converters — designing for efficiency, reliability, and real-world deployability. A particular interest of mine is the intersection of precision motor control with emerging domains: biomedical actuators, prosthetics, and miniaturised drive systems where performance constraints are extreme and the human impact is direct.

Dr. Ankit Dalal
