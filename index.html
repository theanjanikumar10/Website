Domains of Inquiry

Research Areas

Key domains of investigation shaping the future of electric power systems and machine technology.

Electric Machines Design

Analytical and finite-element methods for designing high-efficiency induction motors, permanent magnet machines, and switched reluctance drives.

Motor Control & Drives

Advanced control algorithms including field-oriented control, direct torque control, and model-predictive control for precision variable-speed drives.

Power Electronics

Wide-bandgap device applications, multilevel converter topologies, and EMI mitigation strategies for high-frequency power conversion systems.

Electric Vehicles

Traction motor design, regenerative braking systems, battery-motor co-optimization, and thermal management for next-generation EV powertrains.

From the Lab Head

A Message from the Professor

"The future of sustainable energy depends on our ability to design machines that extract maximum utility from every watt — that challenge drives everything we do here."

Our laboratory sits at the convergence of classical electromagnetic theory and emerging computational methods. We apply finite-element analysis, signal processing, and real-time digital simulation to problems that matter: motors that last longer, drives that waste less, and generators that integrate cleanly with tomorrow's grids.

We welcome collaborations with industry and with peer institutions. If you are a student seeking graduate research opportunities, or an engineer looking to solve a difficult machine design problem, I invite you to reach out.

Dr. Ankit Dalal
Electrical Machines Laboratory. Assnt. Professor, Dept. of Electrical Engineering
Research Interests: PMSM design, motor drives, condition monitoring, renewable energy systems
