    About the Electrical Machines Laboratory

    Established in 2004 within the Department of Electrical Engineering, the Electrical Machines Laboratory has grown from a teaching facility into one of the department's most active research groups. Over two decades, the laboratory has developed deep expertise in the analysis, design, and control of rotating electrical machines and power electronic drive systems.

    Our work spans the full spectrum from fundamental electromagnetic theory and finite-element simulation to experimental validation on industry-standard test rigs. We collaborate with national laboratories, public sector industries, and international research groups to ensure our research addresses real engineering challenges.

    The laboratory plays a central role in undergraduate and postgraduate education, providing students with hands-on experience with motor test equipment, power analyzers, dSPACE real-time controllers, and high-performance workstations running MATLAB/Simulink and ANSYS Maxwell.

    Purpose

    Vision & Mission

    The principles guiding our research, education, and collaboration efforts.

    Vision

    To be a nationally recognised centre of excellence in electrical machines research, producing graduates and knowledge that directly contribute to India's sustainable energy future and industrial competitiveness.

    • Lead research in high-efficiency electric machine design and control
    • Develop engineers equipped for next-generation electrification challenges
    • Bridge the gap between academic theory and industrial application
    • Contribute to national programmes in electric mobility and clean energy

    Mission

    To conduct rigorous, application-driven research in electrical machines and drives while providing students with an educational environment grounded in experimental skill, analytical depth, and professional integrity.

    • Deliver high-quality, hands-on laboratory education at UG and PG levels
    • Publish in high-impact peer-reviewed journals and IEEE conferences
    • Pursue funded projects from DST, SERB, AICTE, and industry partners
    • Mentor doctoral candidates who advance the frontiers of the discipline
    From the Director

    Message from the
    Laboratory Head

    “
    "The physics of rotating electrical machines has not changed — but our ability to design, control, and integrate them with modern energy systems is evolving faster than at any point in the last century."

    When I look at the students and scholars working in this laboratory, I see engineers who understand that precise electromagnetic design and rigorous experimental validation are not in competition with software and simulation — they are what gives simulation its meaning. That philosophy shapes everything we do here.

    Our research agenda is driven by problems that matter: motors that last longer and waste less, drive systems that respond faster and with greater intelligence, generators that integrate cleanly with grids that are increasingly stochastic. These are hard problems. They require people who are willing to sit with complexity and work through it systematically.

    I invite prospective students and collaborators to visit our laboratory, engage with our published work, and reach out. Meaningful research begins with honest conversation.

    Prof. Rajesh Kumar
    Director, Electrical Machines Laboratory · Professor, Dept. of Electrical Engineering
    Research: PMSM design · Motor drives · Condition monitoring · Renewable integration
    rkumar@institute.ac.in
    Infrastructure

    Facilities & Infrastructure

    Experimental resources supporting world-class research and advanced education in electrical machines and drives.

    Electrical Machines Test Bench

    Motor-generator set with torque transducer, speed encoder, and four-quadrant dynamometer for steady-state and dynamic characterisation of AC and DC machines up to 15 kW.

    Efficiency mapping Parameter estimation Load testing

    Motor Drives Laboratory

    Integrated variable-speed drive setup with dSPACE DS1104 real-time controller, IGBT-based three-phase inverter, and interface hardware for implementing FOC, DTC, and MPC strategies.

    FOC / DTC MPC Real-time HIL

    Power Electronics Setup

    Wide-bandgap converter test platform supporting SiC and GaN device evaluation, gate driver design, and multilevel converter topologies for high-frequency and high-efficiency applications.

    SiC / GaN EMI analysis Converter design

    Condition Monitoring System

    NI DAQ-based multi-channel data acquisition with vibration sensors, current probes, and thermal cameras for bearing fault detection, rotor asymmetry diagnosis, and winding insulation testing.

    MCSA Vibration analysis Thermal imaging

    Simulation Workstations

    Eight high-performance workstations running MATLAB/Simulink, ANSYS Maxwell, PLECS, and Python/SciPy for FEM analysis, drive simulation, signal processing, and machine learning workflows.

    FEM design Drive simulation ML / DSP

    Measurement & Instrumentation

    Yokogawa WT power analysers, Fluke LCR meters, high-bandwidth current probes, precision torque transducers, and four-channel digital storage oscilloscopes for accurate machine characterisation.

    Power quality Impedance testing Transient capture

    History

    Achievements & Milestones

    Key events that have shaped the laboratory's identity and research direction over two decades.

    2004
    Laboratory Established Founding

    The Electrical Machines Laboratory was inaugurated within the Department of Electrical Engineering, equipped with induction motor test rigs, DC machine sets, and basic instrumentation for undergraduate teaching.

    2009
    First Major Research Grant — DST SERB Grant

    Secured a ₹28 lakh research grant from DST-SERB to investigate condition monitoring techniques for squirrel cage induction motors using motor current signature analysis. First Ph.D. student enrolled.

    2012
    dSPACE Real-Time Control Platform Acquired

    Installation of the DS1104 R&D controller board enabled the laboratory to implement and validate advanced motor control algorithms in real-time, significantly expanding the scope of experimental research.

    2015
    Industry Collaboration with BHEL Partnership

    A three-year MoU signed with BHEL Bhopal for collaborative research on high-efficiency induction motors for industrial pumps and compressors. Two sponsored Ph.D. positions established.

    2018
    10th Publication Milestone — IEEE Transactions

    The laboratory published its tenth article in IEEE Transactions journals, including IEEE Transactions on Industrial Electronics and IEEE Transactions on Energy Conversion, establishing a consistent record of high-impact publications.

    2020
    National Recognition — INAE Young Engineer Award Award

    Dr. Subhasis Mohanty received the Indian National Academy of Engineering (INAE) Young Engineer Award for outstanding contributions to power electronics and motor drive research.

    2022
    Best Paper Award — IEEE ECCE 2022 Award

    Research on model predictive torque control for three-level NPC inverter-fed PMSM drives won the Best Paper Award at the IEEE Energy Conversion Congress and Exposition, Detroit.

    2024
    EV Research Initiative Launched New

    Launched a dedicated electric vehicle motor and drivetrain research programme with support from AICTE and a major two-wheeler OEM, targeting axial flux motor development and regenerative braking optimisation.
